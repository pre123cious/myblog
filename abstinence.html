ABSTINENCE

What is abstinence

Abstinence means refaining from all forms of sexual intercourse. It also means not engaging in any sexual activities. Abstinence requires a consious decision and efforts to avoid sexual intercourse in order to prevent unwanted pregnancy and sexually transmitted diseases.

How can students stay abstinent

a. Focus on school, work or life goal

b. Wait until you have found the right person

c. Prevent getting STI

d. Desire to avoid unwanted pregnancy

e. Remember why you choose to abstain

f. Think ahead

g. Dont use alcohol or drugs

Reasons for abstinence

a. Focusing on work or education

b. Maintaining moral and religious principles

c. Recovering from illness

d. Waiting for the right person

e. Mourning the loss of a dear person

Behaviours and skills that enhance abstinence

1. To make decisions about abstinence and be determined to carry it out.

2. Communicating sexual limit to one's partners and achieving mutual understanding

3. To reach an agreement on other alternative apart from sex with one's partner to avoid unwanted sexual behaviour

4. Being able to control one's action in sexual situation is necessary

5. Acquiring enough information and knowledge on abstinence

6. To recognize situations that can lead to sexual acts is crucial for the young ones

7. Moving towards the achievement of goals or vision for a successful future.

Benefits of abstinence

1. It is the best method of preventing STI and HIV/AIDS.

2. It is a natural method of preventing unwanted pregnancy

3. It gives assurance of not having to deal with the guilt and other emotional consequences

4. It sets limits in relationship

5. It reduces the risk of crucial cancer

6. It enhances self-esteem.
